Complete Social Media Safety & Digital Citizenship Guide

Navigate the digital world safely and responsibly while protecting your privacy and well-being.

Privacy Settings Examples

Risky: Everything public, location sharing on, accepting all friend requests
Better: Posts visible to friends only, limited personal info displayed
Secure: Restricted profile, location off, carefully curated friend list, no personal details
Expert: Regular privacy audits, separate accounts for different purposes, minimal data sharing
Privacy Protection Essentials
  • Review and adjust privacy settings regularly
  • Turn off location sharing and geotagging
  • Limit who can see your posts and personal info
  • Be selective about friend/follower requests
  • Avoid posting identifying information (address, phone, school)
Stranger Danger Online
  • Never meet strangers from social media in person
  • Don't share personal details with unknown contacts
  • Be wary of overly friendly or persistent strangers
  • Avoid clicking links or downloading files from unknowns
  • Report suspicious or inappropriate behavior immediately
Cyberbullying Prevention
  • Block and report bullies immediately
  • Don't engage or retaliate with harmful content
  • Save evidence (screenshots) of cyberbullying
  • Tell trusted adults or authorities about harassment
  • Support others who are being targeted
Smart Sharing Guidelines
  • Think before you post - is this appropriate and safe?
  • Avoid oversharing personal moments and emotions
  • Consider how posts might affect your future
  • Respect others' privacy in your posts
  • Use the "grandmother rule" - would grandma approve?
Critical Social Media Dangers
  • Digital Predators: Adults who target minors online use manipulation and false identities to build trust.
  • Identity Theft: Oversharing personal information can lead to identity theft and financial fraud.
  • Permanent Records: Everything posted online can be saved, shared, and used against you later.
  • Mental Health Impact: Excessive social media use and cyberbullying can cause anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem.

Advanced Digital Citizenship Strategies

Digital Wellness Balance

Maintain a healthy relationship with social media through mindful usage:

  • Set specific times for social media use (not before bed or first thing in the morning)
  • Use built-in screen time controls and app limits
  • Take regular "digital detox" breaks from social platforms
  • Focus on real-world relationships and activities
Responding to Cyberbullying
  • Document everything with screenshots and timestamps
  • Block the bully on all platforms immediately
  • Report to platform administrators and school officials
  • Seek support from trusted friends, family, or counselors
Protecting Your Digital Footprint
  • Google yourself regularly to see what's public
  • Delete old accounts and inappropriate content
  • Use privacy-focused browsers and search engines
  • Be mindful of what others post about you
Family Communication
  • Establish family social media rules and agreements
  • Have open conversations about online experiences
  • Report concerning interactions to parents/guardians
  • Respect family boundaries about sharing family content
Understanding Platform Policies
  • Read and understand terms of service and privacy policies
  • Know how to report violations and inappropriate content
  • Understand data collection and how your information is used
  • Stay updated on policy changes and new features

Social Media Safety Myths Debunked

Myth: "If my profile is private, I'm completely safe"

Reality: Privacy settings can change, friends might share your content, and platforms can have data breaches. Always be cautious about what you post.

Myth: "Deleting a post means it's gone forever"

Reality: Content can be screenshot, cached, or archived before deletion. Think carefully before posting anything online.

Myth: "Only older adults are online predators"

Reality: Predators can be any age and often pose as peers to gain trust. Always be cautious with anyone you haven't met in person.
